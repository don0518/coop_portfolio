LifeGift

UX, UI, and Interaction Design

Project Context

For this project, I worked on the UX researching that studied potential issues with existing blood donation apps and defined users’ journey of LifeGift. I also designed digital interface and interactions workflow on the following key features: Tracking User’s Accomplishment, Tool Tips, Welcome Page, and Account Login/Setup. (Team size: 5)

Project Type

Academic project – UX, UI study

Role

UI design, UX design & research, Interaction design

Tools

Adobe XD, Illustrator, Photoshop

Stage 1 - Researching

The project begins with researching and understanding of existing blood donation apps, donation policy, and users’ pain points. I interviewed some potential blood donors and regular blood donors by collecting their data and frustrations. After I combined all kinds of raw data from both my interviewee and my team’s works, I was able to create two different user journey maps and personas to identify key points throughout the user’s experience with blood donation. With the data presented, we are able to create an initial wireframe draft for target audiences.

Stage 2 - Creating Digital Wireframes

As the team finalized main features of the app, I was assigned to create digital wireframes and initial workflows for Account Login/Setup. Since LifeGift aimed for a visually attracting and easy access to different information, I followed with the order of hierarchy and applied straight forward sentences for users to quickly understand various information and interaction cues in the page.

Stage 3 - More Researching Before the Refinement

After the team completed the first version of LifeGift, we received meaningful critique from industry designers regarding workflows and visual messages. Before the team begins refining the app, each of us takes the first version to ask feedback from different individuals. At this point, my user testing data was collected from people who had blood donation experience in Canada, people who had blood donation experience outside of Canada, and people who want to donate blood but never did. The reason behind my action is to collect data from potential users and target users which would help the team to minimize the scope of improvement.

Stage 4 - Refining

The previous stage helped us to understand more about our users and acknowledging areas of refinement on each feature. I was assigned to improve Welcome Page, Account Login/Setup, Tracking User’s Accomplishment Page and create Tool Tips for first-time users to understand how to use the app.

Refining Welcome Page

Since the initial design only had minor issues on text size and information provided, I rebuilt content to improve readability. For the Welcome Page, I created multiple infographics to present an overview of LifeGift. These infographics and short descriptions allow users knowing what they would expect in this app and provide a brief introduction of different features.

Refining Account Login/Setup Page

Same as the Welcome page’s initial design, I rebuilt the content’s size to improve readability. In the new Profile Setup Page, I replaced the BC Services Card Number section (see initial wireframe) with Inserting User’s Blood Type. The rationale is to allow users who already know their blood type so the system can collect this information before the donation happens. Besides, users who do not know their blood type can skip this process.

Refining Tracking User’s Accomplishment Page.

Since this page is the key features’ landing page, I intend to make a visually attractive interface by applying an interactive progress circle and intelligible contents. Also, I created different visual milestones to record user’s blood donation progress. While saving the progress, these milestones also give users a sense of achievement after each time of donation which encourages users to donate next time.

Refining Tracking Who You Helped Page

Initially, we had this page as the landing page of Tracking Progress. Although the page is visually attractive, the feature itself seems incomplete - more like a sub-page of a key feature in this app. I took this critique as the inspiration and rebuilt this feature as one of the sub-pages of Tracking User’s Accomplishment Page. I created a new workflow and layout for this page which looks like a sub-feature of Tracking User’s Accomplishment Page. The first page of this sub-feature shows overall donation history and the second page shows the history in detail (such as the location of blood used, thank-you-message from the receiver, etc.). By doing this separation of pages, the user can quickly look through the donation history without confusing by large amounts of information. With helps from teammates, we also created different avatars to represent different receivers that user’s blood used to help (not only for visual but to protect receivers’ privacy).

Adding ToolTips Page.

The ToolTips only happen when a new user login. Since there are four main key features with numbers sub-features, I want to give users a quick starting guide for them to understand what is in the app and how to use it. Users are also allowed to skip this starter guide if they are second time user of this app.

Self Reflection

The result of this project went well and LifeGift was one of the successful apps in the class. However, we had serious critiques from the earlier stage in which LifeGift does not meet its objective, "to solve the issue of low returning rate on blood donation". Besides, the initial interface design did not satisfy users to have a fun, efficient, and informative experience. I found out these issues are mainly caused by the lack of early-stage researching and biasing on users’ experiences. Although the team finally managed and solved issues, the experience shows the importance of researching and analyzing in the early stage.
